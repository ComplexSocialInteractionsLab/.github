# Complex Social Interactions Lab (CSI) — Washington State University

Welcome to the official repository of the **Complex Social Interactions Lab (CSI)** at **Washington State University**.  
CSI conducts empirical research and develops analytic methodologies in behavioral science with a focus on understanding and improving real-world social interactions.

---

## 🧠 About CSI

The **Complex Social Interactions Lab** (CSI) is a research initiative housed within the **Department of Criminal Justice & Criminology** at Washington State University (WSU). CSI is committed to developing **behavioral science methodologies** that address complex interactional phenomena, with a particular emphasis on observable social behaviors involving justice-related contexts.

The lab applies rigorous analytic frameworks to data such as **body-worn camera footage** and observational codebooks to generate insights that can be used by practitioners, policymakers, and researchers alike.

---

## 🎯 Mission

CSI’s mission is to:

- Advance **behavioral science research** grounded in real-world interaction data.  
- Develop methodologies that are **transparent, replicable, and useful** for applied settings.  
- Support evidence-based decision-making across justice, public safety, and community contexts.
- Provide educational and research opportunities to undergraduate and graduate students.

---

## 🧪 Research Activities

CSI’s research encompasses:

- Content analysis of social interaction data (including police–community interactions).
- Development and use of **codebooks** for systematic observation.
- Publications and presentations on interactional behavior and analysis methods.  
- Creation of white papers and other scholarly outputs aimed at improving practice and policy. 

The lab’s research outputs include:
- Peer-reviewed publications  
- Conference presentations  
- Codebooks and training materials  
- White papers illustrating best practices in analysis and observational research
